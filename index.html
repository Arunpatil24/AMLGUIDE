Financial Crime Typologies

Crime Categories & Indicators

Money Laundering — Three Stages

Money laundering is the process of making criminal proceeds appear legitimate. Every case follows three predictable stages, each with distinct transaction signatures.

  • ▸Placement: Dirty cash enters banking system — structuring, smurfing, cash-intensive businesses
  • ▸Layering: Complex transfers obscure origin — wire transfers, shell entities, crypto conversions
  • ▸Integration: Clean money re-enters economy — real estate, business income, investments
  • ▸India Context: PMLA 2002 covers all three stages; FIU-IND receives STRs from all reporting entities
Global Scale
$800B – $2T
Laundered globally every year (UNODC)
Detection Rate
1 – 2%
Of laundered funds ever detected
India STR Deadline
7 Days
From suspicion arising — PMLA requirement

Money Mule Accounts

Mule accounts are used as intermediary channels to receive and forward criminal proceeds — often without the account holder's full awareness.

  • ▸Recruited via fake job ads, social media, or peer pressure
  • ▸Receive funds from multiple unknown payors
  • ▸Immediately forward 90%+ to other accounts — zero retention
  • ▸Keep 5–10% as "commission"
  • ▸Common in UPI fraud, phishing, cyber crime settlement
  • ▸Red flag: new account + rapid pass-through activity
Key Indicator
Pass-Through %
90%+ of inflows immediately transferred out
Common Channel
UPI / IMPS
Instant settlement makes mule networks fast-moving
Account Age Risk
< 6 Months
New accounts showing high velocity are highest risk

Shell Company Activity

Shell companies — entities with no real operations — are the most versatile layering tool in money laundering.

  • ▸Registered in secrecy jurisdictions: BVI, Cayman, Seychelles, Panama
  • ▸Nominee directors disguise true beneficial owner
  • ▸Pay vague "consulting fees," "management charges," "IP royalties"
  • ▸Used in round-tripping, loan-back schemes, and FDI manipulation
  • ▸Red flags: no employees, no physical address, frequent director changes
Common Jurisdictions
BVI · Cayman · Panama
Low/no corporate tax, strong secrecy laws
Detection Challenge
Beneficial Ownership
True owner hidden behind layers of nominee structures

Trade-Based Money Laundering

TBML exploits international trade to move value across borders while evading financial scrutiny.

  • ▸Over-invoicing exports: Receive more than goods are worth
  • ▸Under-invoicing imports: Pay less officially; remainder sent via hawala
  • ▸Phantom shipments: Invoice for goods never shipped
  • ▸Multiple invoicing: Bill same goods in multiple countries
  • ▸Red flags: prices far from LME/market benchmarks
Most Common Method
Over/Under-Invoicing
Price manipulation of goods to shift value across borders
Reference Tool
LME Benchmarking
Compare invoice price vs market price to detect manipulation

Cryptocurrency-Based Crime

Crypto's pseudonymity and speed make it attractive for layering. Blockchain analytics now provides sophisticated tracing tools.

  • ▸Crypto Mixers/Tumblers: Pool and scramble crypto to break transaction trail
  • ▸Chain Hopping: Bitcoin → Monero → Ethereum to obscure trail
  • ▸NFT Wash Trading: Buy/sell NFTs between own wallets at inflated prices
  • ▸DeFi Layering: Decentralized exchanges with no KYC
  • ▸Tools: Chainalysis, Elliptic, CipherTrace for blockchain analytics
Hardest to Trace
Monero (XMR)
Privacy coin with ring signatures — near-zero traceability
India Regulatory
VDA under PMLA
Virtual Digital Assets under PMLA 2023; VASPs must file STRs

CASE · 001 · RETAIL BANKING
Classic Structuring — Vegetable Merchant

Ramesh Gupta, 45. Vegetable merchant. Declared monthly turnover: ₹80K–₹1L. Account age: 8 years. Cooperative Bank, Pune.

Structuring alert. 4 cash deposits in 5 days totalling ₹37.5L. Each: ₹8.8L–₹9.7L — all below ₹10L CTR threshold.

Amounts just below threshold. Deposits at 4 different branches. Total is 37x monthly declared turnover. No prior large deposits in 8 years.

Adverse media linked customer to alleged illegal land deal. Subsequent wire transfers went to shell-like entities. No credible explanation obtained.

⚡ STR Filed with FIU-IND. Account placed under enhanced monitoring. Pattern consistent with structuring + placement stage of money laundering.
CASE · 002 · DIGITAL PAYMENTS
Money Mule — Digital Payments Platform

Anjali Sharma, 24, college student. UPI wallet opened 3 months ago. Min-KYC (Aadhaar OTP only).

Velocity alert — 45 UPI transactions from 38 different VPAs in 4 hours. ₹4,20,000 inflow immediately forwarded to 3 unknown UPI IDs.

Account 3 months old. 38 different senders. Zero fund retention. No organic spending. Sender VPAs linked to phishing complaints on CCRP portal.

Account accessed via same device as two other previously flagged mule accounts. Strong indicator of organized mule network.

⚡ STR Filed. Account suspended. Information shared with CERT-In and Cyber Crime Cell. Customer likely a recruited mule.
CASE · 003 · CORPORATE BANKING
Round-Tripping via Mauritius Route

Sunrise Infrastructure Pvt. Ltd., Mumbai. Construction company, 3 years old. Directors changed twice in 18 months.

₹22Cr sent abroad as "equity investment" to BVI entity. 60 days later ₹23Cr received as "FDI" from Mauritius entity.

Amount and timing suggest same funds returning. FDI purpose stated as "general business development." Director linked to previously flagged entity.

BVI and Mauritius entities share a nominee director. Beneficial owner traced to one of the Indian company's own directors. Classic round-tripping scheme.

⚡ STR Filed. FEMA reporting initiated. ED and SEBI notified per regulatory obligations.
CASE · 004 · FALSE POSITIVE
Dormant NRI Account — Repatriation

Vikram Nair, 38. NRI (UAE). Account dormant 14 months while posted abroad. Returns to India permanently. SBI NRI account.

Dormant account receives ₹28L via SWIFT from Abu Dhabi bank — labeled "repatriation of savings." Followed by FD purchase.

NRI status declared in KYC. UAE bank statement confirms accumulated savings. UAE employment letter provided proactively.

Dormancy explained by overseas posting. SWIFT source is a regulated UAE bank. Destination is FD — not immediate withdrawal. All documentation consistent.

✓ Alert Closed — Fully Explained False Positive. NRI repatriation is a common, commercially logical life event.
CASE · 005 · CRYPTO
Crypto Ransomware Proceeds

Arjun Mehta, 29. Freelance cryptocurrency trader. Monthly activity typically ₹50K–₹80K.

₹1.2Cr received from a VDA exchange over 3 weeks. Immediately transferred to 8 different bank accounts. Volume 15x prior baseline.

Crypto holdings accumulated within 10 days. Funds passed through a known crypto mixer. Originating wallet linked to ransomware attack by Chainalysis.

8 receiving bank accounts were newly opened by unrelated individuals — confirmed mule network. Arjun's account used as conversion layer.

⚡ STR Filed. Account suspended. Exchange notified. Referred to cybercrime cell as potential ransomware proceeds.
CASE · 006 · UNDER REVIEW
Real Estate Agent — Anomalous Commission

Harish Malhotra, 48. Real estate broker, Gurugram. Commission-based income. Account 5 years old. Risk: Medium-High.

₹25L in cash deposited over 3 days. Stated reason: "commission from property sale."

Property sale: ₹3.2Cr. Standard 2% commission = ₹64,000. ₹25L commission = 7.8% — commercially unusual. No registered commission agreement provided.

Story partially plausible. Cash format and rate are both flagged. Additional documentation requested under CDD review.

⏳ Escalated to Level 2. Awaiting commission agreement and counterparty confirmation. 7-day STR clock running.
